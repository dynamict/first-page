Count on Me

Let's learn about my involvement with Count on Me!

Count on Me is one of the earliest leadership experiences I had in university. Being only in my second year, I was able to lead a team of 16 other student volunteers to help the community through teaching financial literacy, healthy eating, and employability skills.

What is Count on Me?


Count on Me is a social entrepreneurship program geared towards at-risk youth and young mothers in the Lower Mainland. It caters to them through teaching them financial literacy, employability skills, and healthy eating habits over an 8 week program. With each week, participants learn new skills aimed to help them save money and live healthier lifestyles through interactive classes to raise class engagement and information retention.


Operating in 3 cities in 2017, Count on Me continues to grow through expanding into different municipalities and is in the process of moving its curriculum online to expand its reach nationally.


Count on Me was created when we identified a problem in students and young adults where they would have bad spending habits or a lack of understanding when it came to financial literacy. After initially creating a program that focused on financial literacy, we recognized that more was needed in the curriculum was needed for our participants to feel comfortable with their new-found knowledge. For instance, our initial curriculum taught about the importance of spending less on eating out and fast food. However, students did not know how to cook their own food and were not comfortable with buying groceries.
