Count on Me

Let's learn about my involvement with Count on Me!

Count on Me is one of the earliest leadership experiences I had in university. Being in my second year, I led a team of 16 student volunteers to help the community through teaching financial literacy, healthy eating, and employability skills.

What is Count on Me?


Count on Me is a social entrepreneurship program geared towards at-risk youth and young mothers in the Lower Mainland. It caters to them through teaching financial literacy, employability skills, and healthy eating habits over an 8 week program. With each week, participants learn new skills aimed to help them save money and live healthier lifestyles through interactive classes to raise engagement and information retention.


Operating in 3 cities in 2017, Count on Me continues to grow through expanding into different municipalities and is in the process of moving its curriculum online to expand its reach nationally.


Count on Me was created when we identified a problem in students and young adults where they would have a lack of financial literacy. After initially creating a program that focused on financial literacy, we recognized that more was needed in the curriculum for our participants to feel comfortable with their new-found knowledge. For instance, our initial curriculum taught about the importance of spending less on eating out and fast food. However, students did not know how to cook their own food and were not comfortable with buying groceries. Therefore, we improved the curriculum to include topics such as a hands-on healthy eating and employability skills components.






Leading the Team

Leading a team of 16 other student volunteers, we contributed of 2,200 hours of Count on Me support to the communities that we love. With roles ranging from acquiring sponsorship packages to leading the workshops, I was able to support each student volunteer with the resources they needed to not only succeed, but have fun while doing so!
