SFU Marketing Accelerator Program

Let's learn about SFU's Marketing Accelerator Program

or click here to see the marketing for it!

For its 1st year, SFU's Beedie School of Business has its own program dedicated to helping you launch a career in the field of Marketing. SFU’s Marketing Accelerator Program (MAP) is a 9-week program that covers everything from acing behavioural/case interviews, gaining practical marketing skills, exclusive networking opportunities and first-hand marketing experiences.

The idea for MAP started from the revelation that marketing students at SFU did not have a clear vision of how they would achieve the level of success they dreamed of. While students going consulting and investment banking have separate programs connecting them with professionals in their respective fields and provide an in-depth curriculum teaching students the ins and outs of the industry, marketing students did not have such resources to push students to the next level. This is where MAP was born, to truly support the best marketing students at SFU to reach their full potential and land the jobs that they want to land.


What is MAP?


MAP dives into different aspects of the marketing world to help students understand which avenue of marketing they will pursue as their career. Through providing pro-bono marketing work for start-up companies and having industry experts leading seminars, MAP participants are able to accelerate their path to marketing success.


Not only do MAP participants learn about marketing specific topics, but they are also trained to perform better in interviews through three rounds of mock interviews with industry professionals in the marketing field. Mock interviews help students learn the full spectrum of knowledge needed to ace their real interviews, including training in both behavioural and case interviews.

Program Curriculum

To create the 9-week curriculum, we wanted to ensure that the lessons MAP participants learned were relevant and in high demand for today’s business market. Therefore, we based our curriculum off Google’s Digital Marketing Certificate. This provided participants with something tangible that they could work towards and display as an achievement for themselves.

Through including so many different topics for MAP participants to be introduced to, we sought to supplement certain weeks with extra material. These materials included having industry speakers coming in to shine extra light on the topics at hand and also providing pro-bono marketing services to local start-ups so MAP participants can gain hands-on experience working with topics such as SEO.
