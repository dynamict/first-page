Leadership Experiences

Let's take a look at the times where I led a team!

Recognizing issues and creating initiatives to combat those problems is something that I have consistently done thorughout my university career. Whether it's focused on the the greater community or my student peers, come check out some of the teams I've led!


SFU Marketing Accelerator Program

SFU’s Marketing Accelerator Program (MAP) is a 9-week program that covers everything from acing behavioural/case interviews, gaining practical marketing skills, exclusive networking opportunities, and first-hand marketing experiences.

Key Contributions:

1. Researched key marketing trends in today's marketing industry and created the curriculum used to guide participants through the MAP course.

2. Instructed MAP participants on how to effectively analyze and strategize case studies.

3. Managed relationships with stakeholders for in-person/online presentations to connect with students based on specific marketing industry focus areas.

Key Metrics:

• Partnered with 14 companies to provide seminars and presentations, providing in-depth industry insights

• Provided pro-bono marketing services to 5 start-up companies, allowing MAP participants to practice newly learned skills

• Currently supporting MAP participants in securing full-time/internship positions

Impact: Successfully created a network that connects the top SFU marketing students with marketing professionals and marketing recruiters. Also, provided pro-bono marketing work to 5 Vancouver startups to provide MAP participants with hands-on marketing experience.


Count on Me

Count on Me (CoM) is one of the earliest leadership experiences I had in university. Being only in my second year, I was able to lead a team of 16 other student volunteers to help the community through teaching financial literacy, healthy eating, and employability skills.

Key Contributions:

1. Successfully acquired three partnerships in different cities for Count on Me's inaugural year

2. Experimented and revamped the curriculum to continuously include relevant information catered to the audience it was being presented to

3. Rebranded Count on Me to fit the needs of the target demographic's consumption behaviours



Key Metrics:

• 90+ students impacted in 3 different municipalities

• Over $25,000 saved/invested among students

• Received $3,000 in sponsorship funding

• Managed a team of 16 volunteers to contribute over 2,200 volunteer hours

Impact: Through running the program, Count on Me was named the Best Financial Literacy program in Western Canada and the 2nd Best Financial Literacy program nationwide
